# Yama Brand Guidelines

## Brand Overview

Yama modernizes existing dialer-intercom systems with cloud-based smart features for building access management. The brand emphasizes innovation, clarity, accessibility, and human connection through technology.

---

## Logo System

### Primary Logo
- Full wordmark "YAMA" in pixel-style geometric letterforms
- Use for maximum brand visibility: interfaces, print materials, marketing assets

### Secondary Logo / Icon
- Standalone "Y" logomark
- Use for compact placements: app icons, favicons, social media profiles, merchandise

### Stacked Logo
- Two-line arrangement: "YA" over "MA"
- Use for spatial efficiency in small-scale branding

### Logo Specifications
- **Minimum size**: 140px width / 35px height
- **Clear space**: Maintain consistent open space around logo; no other elements should intrude
- **Never**: Stretch, distort, change spacing, or use non-approved colors

### Logo on Photography
- Ensure sufficient background contrast for legibility
- Use white logo on dark/busy backgrounds

## Typography

### Typeface: Manrope
A modern geometric sans-serif designed for clarity and accessibility.

| Weight | Usage |
|--------|-------|
| **Bold** | Headings, emphasis |
| **Regular** | Body text |
| **Light** | Large layouts, subtle emphasis |

### Hierarchy Examples
- **Heading**: Bold, largest size
- **Title**: "Say Goodbye to Your Entryway Headaches"
- **Subtitle**: "Even your light bulbs are smart these days so why can't your intercom be, too?"
- **Body**: Regular weight for readable information delivery

## Visual Language

### Design Direction
- Geometric structure with clarity and flow
- Balances technology precision with human connection
- Modular, pixel-like connections forming unified systems

### Pattern System
- Built from rounded geometric units derived from logo grid
- Represents connection and flow
- Symbolizes linking people, buildings, and digital systems
- Use to support design without dominating; maintain visual balance

### Pattern Usage
- Can be applied as masks over photography
- Works as background texture
- Effective for creating visual interest on marketing materials
